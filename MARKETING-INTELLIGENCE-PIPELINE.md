# Marketing Intelligence Pipeline for OpenDash

**Purpose**: Convert competitive intelligence into actionable marketing strategy
**Status**: Framework document
**Last Updated**: 2026-03-24

---

## Overview

The **Marketing Intelligence Pipeline** takes data from the Competitor Intelligence System and converts it into OpenDash marketing decisions, content, positioning, and strategy.

```
COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE
        ↓
    ANALYSIS
        ↓
MARKETING DECISIONS
        ↓
CONTENT STRATEGY
        ↓
POSITIONING & MESSAGING
        ↓
MARKETING EXECUTION
        ↓
PERFORMANCE TRACKING
```

---

## Part 1: Intelligence → Marketing Decisions

### Decision Framework

For each significant competitor finding, ask:

```
1. THREAT ASSESSMENT
   └─ Is this a threat to OpenDash?
   ├─ Directly competitive? (Same market, features)
   ├─ Could hurt our market position? (Messaging, features)
   ├─ Urgent response needed? (Yes/No)
   └─ Risk level: CRITICAL / HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW

2. OPPORTUNITY ANALYSIS
   └─ Can we learn from this?
   ├─ Better positioning? (How they message differently)
   ├─ Features to prioritize? (What's working for them)
   ├─ Market gap they're filling? (We could fill too)
   └─ Opportunity level: HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW

3. DECISION POINT
   └─ What should we do?
   ├─ Copy/Adapt their approach?
   ├─ Differentiate against them?
   ├─ Ignore and focus on our roadmap?
   ├─ Accelerate/Pivot our strategy?
   └─ Decision: [Action + Timeline]

4. EXECUTION
   └─ What specific actions?
   ├─ Content to create?
   ├─ Messaging to change?
   ├─ Features to highlight?
   ├─ Campaigns to run?
   └─ Owner + Timeline
```

├─ Positioning: Integration + Flexibility
├─ Messaging: "One dashboard for all your data"

RECOMMENDATION: Combination of #2 + #3
├─ Primary: "AI-native analytics for lean teams"

## Part 4: Content Strategy from Competitor Analysis

### Content Gap Analysis

**Process**:

```
1. AUDIT COMPETITOR CONTENT
   ├─ What topics do they cover?
   ├─ How deep do they go?
   ├─ What formats? (Blog, tutorial, case study, doc)
   ├─ How frequently published?
   └─ What's their SEO strategy?

2. IDENTIFY GAPS
   ├─ What topics do ALL competitors avoid?
   ├─ Where's weak content quality?
   ├─ What underserved needs?
   └─ What questions aren't answered?

3. PRIORITIZE OPENINGS
   ├─ High volume + Low competition topics
   ├─ Topics that align with our positioning
   ├─ Topics we're uniquely qualified to teach
   └─ Topics that drive leads for our specific market

4. BUILD CONTENT CALENDAR
   └─ Create content competitors miss
```

### Example Content Strategy

**Competitor Content Analysis**:

```
Metabase Blog Topics:
├─ Business intelligence basics
├─ SQL tutorials
├─ BI best practices
├─ Case studies (how customers use it)
└─ Product announcements

Missing:
├─ "BI for non-technical founders" (only for professionals)
├─ "Analytics without SQL" (just assumes you know it)
├─ "Choosing between BI tools" (obviously biased)
└─ "Lean startup analytics" (only enterprise focus)

OpenDash Content Opportunities:
├─ "BI for First-Time Founders" (deep, beginner-friendly)
├─ "5-Minute Analytics Setup Guide" (unique angle)
├─ "Founder's Dashboard 101" (niche, unique)
├─ "How to Make Data Decisions in 2 Weeks"
├─ "AI-Powered Analytics: What Actually Works"
└─ "Running a Data-Driven Startup on a $2K Budget"
```

### Content Types to Prioritize

Based on competitive advantage:

```
FORMAT 1: Tutorials & How-To Guides
├─ Why: Competitors do this, but generic
├─ Our Advantage: Make it founder-specific
├─ Example: "How to Set Up Your First Marketing Dashboard"
├─ SEO Value: HIGH (how-to = high search volume)
└─ Lead Value: MEDIUM (attracts people exploring tools)

FORMAT 2: Founder/Lean-Focused Case Studies
├─ Why: Most case studies are enterprise
├─ Our Advantage: Relatable founder stories
├─ Example: "How IndieHacker Studios Built Analytics in 1 Day"
├─ SEO Value: MEDIUM
└─ Lead Value: HIGH (attracts target audience)

FORMAT 3: Educational Content (Positioning)
├─ Why: Educate market on why our approach matters
├─ Our Advantage: AI + simplicity messaging
├─ Example: "Why AI Will Revolutionize BI for Non-Technical Founders"
├─ SEO Value: MEDIUM-HIGH
└─ Lead Value: MEDIUM (builds credibility)

FORMAT 4: Comparison Content
├─ Why: High volume searchers
├─ Our Advantage: Founder-focused comparisons
├─ Example: "Metabase vs OpenDash: Which for Founders?"
├─ SEO Value: VERY HIGH (high commercial intent)
└─ Lead Value: VERY HIGH (captures in-market buyers)

FORMAT 5: Product Updates & Feature Stories
├─ Why: Keeps people updated (all competitors do this)
├─ Our Advantage: Frame features around use cases
├─ Example: "AI Chat Now Understands Your Business Logic"
├─ SEO Value: LOW (branded)
└─ Lead Value: MEDIUM (keeps engaged audience informed)

PRIORITY: #2 + #4 (case studies + comparisons)
└─ Most aligned with market opportunity
└─ Highest lead generation potential
└─ Differentiated from generic competitor content
```

## Part 8: Monitoring Competitive Threats & Opportunities

### Red Flags to Watch

```
THREAT SIGNALS:

Price Reduction:
└─ Competitor drops price significantly → They're struggling for adoption
└─ Action: Watch if market reacts, may need to adjust positioning

Feature Parity:
└─ Competitor adds feature OpenDash is known for
└─ Action: Either copy quickly or lean into different positioning

Messaging Shift:
└─ Competitor changes main positioning
└─ Action: Analyze why, could signal market shift

Major Funding:
└─ Competitor raises big round
└─ Action: Expect aggressive marketing, feature acceleration

Product Launches:
└─ Competitor launches new product in adjacent market
└─ Action: Evaluate if we should follow or defend

Leadership Changes:
└─ CEO/CTO changes, new leadership team
└─ Action: Watch for strategy shifts, cultural changes
```

### Opportunity Signals

```
OPPORTUNITY SIGNALS:

Messaging Confusion:
└─ Competitor lacks clear positioning
└─ Opportunity: Own clearer position

Feature Gaps:
└─ All competitors missing specific feature
└─ Opportunity: Be first mover on important feature

Underserved Segment:
└─ Competitor ignores segment (e.g., founders, non-technical)
└─ Opportunity: Dominate niche

Content Weakness:
└─ Competitor has weak blog/education
└─ Opportunity: Win on educational content

Community Gaps:
└─ Competitor doesn't engage with community well
└─ Opportunity: Build strong community advantage
```

## Part 11: Quick Decision Guide

### "A Competitor Just Did X" - Decision Framework

```
SCENARIO 1: Competitor Launched Major Feature

Questions:
1. Does it directly compete with OpenDash?
2. Can customers replicate it with OpenDash?
3. Is it core to their differentiation?
4. Can we implement similar feature quickly?

Decision Tree:
├─ If "our customers need this immediately"
│  └─ Action: Prioritize feature in next sprint
├─ If "customers might switch for this"
│  └─ Action: Plan feature, communicate roadmap
├─ If "different market focus"
│  └─ Action: Monitor but don't change roadmap
└─ If "minor/niche feature"
   └─ Action: Track for future, no immediate response

Timeline: Decision within 1 business day
```

```
SCENARIO 2: Competitor Changed Pricing

Questions:
1. Did they go up or down?
2. What triggered the change?
3. Is our pricing still competitive?
4. Will this hurt our sales?

Decision Tree:
├─ If "significantly underpriced compared to market"
│  └─ Action: Evaluate pricing strategy change
├─ If "new value-based pricing"
│  └─ Action: Consider matching approach
├─ If "temporary promotion"
│  └─ Action: Match if needed, don't change strategy
└─ If "no material threat"
   └─ Action: Document, monitor market response

Timeline: Decision within 2 business days
```

```
SCENARIO 3: Competitor Changed Messaging

Questions:
1. Did they find a better positioning?
2. Does it now compete more directly with us?
3. Are customers responding positively?
4. Should we adjust our messaging?

Decision Tree:
├─ If "more effective positioning than ours"
│  └─ Action: Analyze, consider messaging refresh
├─ If "different market, no threat to us"
│  └─ Action: Document, stay focused
├─ If "same positioning, better execution"
│  └─ Action: Improve our messaging/execution
└─ If "temporary/marketing experiment"
   └─ Action: Wait and see if sticks

Timeline: Decision within 3-5 business days (more analysis needed)
```
